What is Food Guides?

A Food Guides is a visual plan that provides recommended types and portions of food to help people maintain a balanced, healthy diet and build lifelong habits for well-being and disease prevention.

Study Food Guide

Study Food Guide is a curated list or plan of foods and drinks that help improve your concentration and mental energy while studying.

List of Food and Drinks for Studying (Focus, Memory, Energy)

Foods:

DARK CHOCOLATE

It contains flavonoids, caffeine, and antioxidants that boost concentration and mood.

Tips:

  • Choose at least 70% cocoa and eat in moderation.
  • Limit to 1-2 squares per serving - Great for focus without overindulging.

    • WALNUTS

    Good healthy fats, magnesium, and zinc sources for brain health and focus.

    Tips:

  • Keep a trail mix or snack bag nearby while studying.
  • Add to salads, yogurt, or oatmeal for texture and brain-boosting omega-3s.

    • SALMON

    High in omega-3 fatty acids that support brain function and cognitive performance.

    Tips:

  • Eat grilled or baked; even canned tuna is a decent option.
  • Serve with greens or brown rice for a balanced, brain-supportive meal.

    • Drinks:

    COFFEE

    Caffeine helps with alertness and short-term memory.

    Tips:

  • Limit to 1-2 cups per session to avoid crashes or anxiety.
  • Best for morning or early afternoon, Avoid close to bedtime to protect sleep.

    • SMOOTHIES

    Offer a balance of antioxidants, fiber, and natural sugars for energy.

    Tips:

  • Blend banana, berries, and almond milk for a brain-boosting drink.
  • Add healthy fats like peanut butter, flax seeds, or avocado.

    • WATER

    Dehydration can lead to fatigue and poor concentration.

    Tips:

  • Drink at least 6 to 8 glasses a day. More if you're active or in warm climates.
  • Start and end your day with water, Supports digestion and reduces brain fog.

    • Stress Food Guide

    Stress Food Guide includes foods and drinks by NutriCraves will guide and help your body and mind.

    List of Food and Drinks for Stress Relief (Calming, Soothing, Balancing)

    Foods:

    AVOCADO TOAST

    Packed with B vitamins and healthy fats that support brain function and reduce anxiety.

    Tips:

  • Add to toast or salads for a satisfying and calming meal.
  • Half an avocado per toast is plenty (it's healthy but calorie-dense).

    • OATS

    A good source of complex carbs and melatonin precursors for better sleep and calm.

    Tips:

  • Overnight oats are convenient; cooked oats are warm and comforting.
  • Mix in Greek yogurt, nuts, fruits, or protein powder to stay full longer.

    • KIMCHI RICE

    Support gut health, which is linked to mood regulation and anxiety reduction.

    Tips:

  • Eat a few tablespoons a day to let your gut adjust to probiotics.
  • Serve with rice, eggs, or noodles to balance its strong flavor.

    • Drinks:

    CHAMOMILE TEA

    Known for its calming and sleep-inducing properties.

    Tips:

  • Best enjoyed before bed or during high-stress moments.
  • Add honey or lemon for natural sweetness and extra relaxation.

    • WARM MILK

    It contains tryptophan, which helps promote relaxation and sleep.

    Tips:

  • Add a touch of honey or cinnamon for extra comfort.
  • Drink 30 minutes before bed can help trigger sleepiness.

    • COCONUT WATER

    Rich in electrolytes that can support hydration and reduce tension.

    Tips:

  • Chill it for a refreshing stress-relief drink.
  • Sip slowly during tense moments the mild sweetness and cooling effect can help calm your nervous system.
